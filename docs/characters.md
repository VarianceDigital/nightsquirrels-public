# Characters for the UI/UX

## Core synthesis

The UI/UX is enriched by 5 characters.
Four squirrels: Ale (F), Bea (F), Cosmo (M) and Dario (M). And Leo, old lion, owner of the book wall library.

**Leo** is the guardian of knowledge; the 4 **squirrels** are the inventors of trajectories.

If the squirrels invent trajectories, they belong in the foreground.
If Leo guards knowledge, he can inhabit the background like a constant, stabilizing presence.

### Leo

Role
The old lion-scholar. Owner of the Book Wall. Vast experience, broad knowledge, connecting things.

Visual traits

elderly but solid
white beard
square glasses with thin frames (eyeglasses, not reading glasses)
calm posture

slightly scholarly untidy, reading, observing
expression frequently thoughtful and calm, sometimes surprised

occasionally impressed by the squirrels’ daring

Signature dynamic
Leo often knows why something matters.
The squirrels often discover how to get there.

### The squirrel team as a whole

Collective role
They are the living energy of the platform: quick, smart, resourceful, diverse.
They are not subordinate mascots. They are a distributed intelligence.

What they symbolize

practical problem-solving
courage
curiosity
many paths to understanding
youthful competence
mutual support

### Squirrel Ale — the bold female trailblazer

Reaches the right book first, acts fast under pressure. 
Social intelligence.

Core traits
quick-minded
decisive
playful, but not frivolous
chill and sweet
simple beauty

Why she matters
She gives young female students an identification figure who is not passive, ornamental, or merely “nice.”
She is strong because she acts, solves, dares.

Relationship with Leo
She is one of the main sources of Leo’s “slight surprise.”
Not because she is reckless, but because she sees unconventional paths, and because
she connects people and things.


### Squirrel Bea — the precise female analyst

This second female squirrel is useful so the first one does not carry all female representation by herself.

Role
The squirrel of structure, precision, patterns, classification, logic, elegant solutions.

Core traits

analytical
composed
exact
observant
quietly formidable

Why she matters
She broadens the spectrum of female identification:
not only bold and action-driven, but also rigorous, elegant, deeply smart.

Visual cues

neat gestures
attention to arrangement, maps, indexes, diagrams
perhaps the squirrel most at home near charts, notes, labels, or formulae

Relationship with Leo
Leo trusts her sense of structure.

### Squirrel Cosmo — the inventive male improviser

Role
Fast-thinking, experimental, slightly eccentric, brilliant in lateral solutions.

Core traits

imaginative
witty
improvisational
energetic
inventive

What he represents

creative problem-solving
experimentation
non-linear thinking
productive play

Best narrative function
He turns obstacles into mechanisms.

### Squirrel Dario — the patient male supporter

Role
The dependable, careful, perhaps less flashy squirrel who stabilizes the group.

Core traits

patient
kind
detail-oriented
persistent
reliable

Why he matters
Not all users identify with daring brilliance. Some identify with steadiness, care, and persistence. This squirrel makes the world psychologically broader.

Best narrative function
He finishes what others begin.

IMPORTANT: Ale and Dario are deeply connected, they love each other naturally. 

## The key dynamic between Leo and the squirrels

The ideal relationship is not:

master vs assistants,
elder vs children,
teacher vs mascots.

It is more like:

Leo holds the map of the world.
The squirrels discover new lines across the map.
